The menu

What an Argentine asado actually looks like.

Below is the full repertoire. We don't serve all of it at every event — instead, we build your menu around the size of your group, what you've eaten before and what you want to discover. Three set menus are listed further down for reference.

We'll cook every cut to the doneness your table prefers.

Raw Rare Medium-rare Medium Well done

Picada · before the grill

Provoleta provoleta

Grilled provolone disc with oregano and chili flakes. The Argentine welcome.

Salame & cheese board tabla

Cured Argentine-style salame, manchego, olives, pickled vegetables, crusty bread.

Empanadas empanadas

Hand-folded beef empanadas, baked on the grill. Two per guest.

First fire · achuras — hover for grill temp

Chorizo criollo chorizo

Fresh pork sausage with garlic and red pepper. Served as choripán with chimichurri.

Morcilla morcilla

Argentine blood sausage. For the curious — it's softer and sweeter than you expect.

Mollejas mollejas

Sweetbreads, slow-grilled until crisp outside, custard-soft inside. Add-on.

Chinchulines chinchulines

Braided small intestine, grilled crisp with lemon. Add-on for the adventurous.

The cuts · carnes — hover for grill temp

Asado de tira asado de tira

Short rib cut across the bone, slow-cooked Argentine-style. The signature.

Vacío vacío

Flank steak with a crisp fat cap. Soft, juicy, the crowd favorite.

Entraña entraña

Skirt steak — intense, marbled, served rare to medium. Argentinian steakhouse staple.

Picaña picaña

Top sirloin cap. The cut that travels — even non-meat-people come back for seconds.

Bife de chorizo bife de chorizo

Strip loin steak, thick cut. Optional upgrade for steakhouse fans.

Pollo a la parrilla pollo

Free-range chicken, butterflied and grilled with chimichurri. Light option.

On the side · guarniciones

Chimichurri chimichurri

House-made, parsley, garlic, oregano, vinegar, olive oil. On every table.

Salsa criolla criolla

Diced tomato, onion, pepper, vinegar. Bright, fresh, cuts the fat.

Grilled vegetables verduras

Zucchini, eggplant, peppers, onion, grilled over embers with olive oil.

Mozzarella & tomato caprese

Slow-ripened tomato, fresh mozzarella, basil, oil. For the vegetarians.

Papas a la crema papas

Roasted potatoes with cream, garlic and herbs.

Mixed leaf salad ensalada

Rocket, lettuce, tomato, onion, lemon, oil.

After the fire · postre

Flan with dulce de leche flan mixto

Vanilla flan crowned with dulce de leche and a swirl of cream. The classic.

Panqueque de dulce de leche panqueque

Crepe filled with dulce de leche, flambéed at the table with rum.

Espresso & fernet café · fernet

The closing ritual. Strong coffee, herbal digestif over ice with cola.

The cellar · bebidas

Argentine Malbec vino tinto

Mendoza Malbec, the natural pairing for grilled beef. House selection.

Spanish red tinto español

Rioja or Ribera del Duero, an alternative for the table.

White & rosé blanco · rosado

Verdejo, Albariño or Argentine Torrontés. Penedès rosé.

Beer cerveza

Estrella Damm, Moritz, plus a couple of Catalan craft options.

Soft drinks & water aguas

Coca-Cola, Sprite, tonic, still and sparkling water.

Mate mate

Argentine yerba mate, traditional gourd and bombilla, on request.

The Classic

El Clásico

The Argentine staples, end to end. Best for first-timers and groups of 6–20.

El Parrillero
