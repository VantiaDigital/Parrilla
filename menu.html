The menu

What an Argentine asado actually looks like.

Below is the full repertoire. We don't serve all of it at every event — instead, we build your menu around the size of your group, what you've eaten before and what you want to discover. Three set menus are listed further down for reference.

Choose your doneness

Drag the slider — how do you like your meat?

We'll cook every cut to the doneness your table prefers.

Raw Rare Medium-rare Medium Well done

The asado · carnes — hover for grill temp

Asado de tira asado de tira

Short rib cut across the bone, slow-cooked Argentine-style. The signature.

Vacío vacío

Flank steak with a crisp fat cap. Soft, juicy, the crowd favourite.

Entraña entraña

Skirt steak — intense, marbled, served rare to medium.

With the asado · acompañamientos

Bread pan

Fresh local bread, served with every plate. The other half of a good asado.

Chimichurri chimichurri

House-made: parsley, garlic, oregano, vinegar, olive oil. On every table.

Salsa criolla salsa criolla

Diced tomato, onion, pepper, vinegar. Bright, fresh, cuts the fat.

Vegetable skewers pinchos de verdura

Zucchini, peppers, onion, mushroom, eggplant — threaded and grilled over embers.

Whole grilled vegetables verduras enteras caseras

Whole onion, pepper, sweet potato or eggplant, slow-roasted in the embers. Homemade.

Drinks · bebidas — canned, cold, no fuss

Water agua

Still and sparkling, in cans or bottles. Plenty for the table.

Soft drinks refrescos

Coca-Cola, Sprite, Fanta and others — by the can.

Beer cerveza

Estrella Damm, Moritz, Mahou — cold cans, kept on ice.

Canned cocktails tragos en lata

Ready-to-drink: gin tonic, vodka tonic, rum & coke, mojito, fernet con cola.

Ice hielo

Plenty of it. Keeps everything cold all afternoon.

Service · all disposable

Plates, cutlery, cups, napkins

Everything is single-use disposable. We bring it all, take it all away — you don't wash a single thing.
