About us

Two brothers.
One grill, since we were kids.

We're Bruno and Facu Goette. We grew up in Argentina watching our father light a parrilla every Sunday, the way most kids watch cartoons. We moved to Barcelona to study and stayed because the city felt good — but the Sunday asado came with us.

The origin

Sunday lunch,
turned into a business.

For the first two years in Barcelona we cooked for friends — Argentines missing home, Spanish neighbors curious about the smoke coming off the terrace, Erasmus kids who had never seen a chorizo bitten right off the grill.

Word kept spreading. People started asking us to cook for birthdays. Then a wedding. Then a couple of summer-rental groups from the US who wanted "the local thing, but real". We bought a proper portable parrilla, then a second one, and named ourselves what our friends already called us: Los Hermanos Parrilleros.

Today we cook all over Barcelona and the Catalan coast. The job hasn't changed much. We arrive, we light the fire, we feed the table, we leave it clean.

How we work

The rules we cook by.

I

Real fire

Charcoal, never gas. Embers, not flame. Some cuts cook in 8 minutes, others in three hours. We respect the difference.

II

Real meat

We source from a single butcher in Mercabarna who supplies Argentine restaurants in the city. Dry-aged where it matters. No shortcuts.

III

Hosting, not catering

We don't show up in branded vans and disappear. We sit with the table when the meat is on, explain the cuts, pour the wine. It's part of the experience.

IV

Leave it cleaner

You should not be able to tell we were there — except for the dulce de leche in the fridge.

We're not a restaurant that comes to your house.
We're the Sunday lunch you wish your friends in Argentina had cooked for you.
— Bruno & Facu
