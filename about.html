About us

Two brothers. one grill. since we were kids.

BRUNO & FACUNDO
— Goette · Hermanos · Asadores —

We're Bruno and Facundo Goette. We grew up in Rosario, Argentina, watching our father light a parrilla every Sunday, the way most kids watch cartoons. He taught us in the slow way — which cuts forgive a mistake, which ones don't, how long the embers take, when to pour wine and when to wait.

We moved to Barcelona to study and stayed because the city felt good. But the Sunday asado came with us, and the first year here it was just for ourselves — homesick Argentines on a 4 × 3 m terrace, neighbours knocking on the door asking what's that smell.

"We're not a restaurant that
comes to your house —
we're the Sunday lunch."

Friends asked us to cook for their birthdays. Then a wedding. Then a couple of summer-rental groups from the US who wanted "the local thing, but real." We bought a proper portable parrilla, then a second one, and named ourselves what our friends already called us: Los Hermanos Parrilleros.

Today we cook all over Barcelona and the Catalan coast — terraces in Eixample, beach plots in Garraf, masías in Penedès, country houses in Empordà. The job hasn't changed much. We arrive, we light the fire, we feed the table, we leave it clean. That's the work.

How we work

The rules we cook by.

I

Real fire

Charcoal, never gas. Embers, not flame. Some cuts cook in 8 minutes, others in three hours. We respect the difference.

II

Real meat

We source from a single butcher in Mercabarna who supplies Argentine restaurants in the city. Dry-aged where it matters.

III

Hosting, not catering

We don't show up in branded vans and disappear. We sit with the table, explain the cuts, pour the wine. It's part of the experience.

IV

Leave it cleaner

You should not be able to tell we were there — except for the dulce de leche left in the fridge.

We're not a restaurant that comes to your house.
We're the Sunday lunch you wish your friends
in Argentina had cooked for you.
— Bruno & Facundo
